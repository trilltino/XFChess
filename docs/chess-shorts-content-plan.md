# Chess Shorts Content Plan

## Goal
Produce short-form chess video content that maximizes views, watch time, and follower growth across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels.

---

## Content Tier Rankings (by engagement)

| Rank | Content Type | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 1 | Tactical Puzzles | 2.3x higher engagement than other types; viewers stay for the answer |
| 2 | Blunders & Dramatic Moments | Non-players engage; meme-able; instantly emotional |
| 3 | Celebrity / Streamer Matchups | Magnus, Hikaru, BotezLive clips — aspirational + entertaining |
| 4 | Tournament Highlights | OTB drama, endgame finishes, time pressure moments |
| 5 | Trick Openings | Appeals to beginners wanting quick wins |

---

## The Winning Formula

```
Strong Hook (0–3s)
  ↓
Tension / Puzzle Setup (3–12s)
  ↓
Dramatic Reveal or Checkmate (12–19s)
  ↓
CTA — "follow for daily puzzles" or "could you find it?"
```

**Target length:** 8–19 seconds for max completion rate. Never exceed 60s unless storytelling demands it.

---

## Platform Breakdown

### TikTok
- **Best content:** Blunders, puzzles, trending audio sync
- **Hook window:** First 3 seconds — visual shock or bold text overlay
- **Algorithm signal:** Completion rate + shares
- **Tips:**
  - Sync piece moves to viral audio beats
  - Use #ChessTok #Checkmate hashtags
  - Show a visually striking board setup in the first frame
  - Dramatic slow-motion checkmates perform especially well

### YouTube Shorts
- **Best content:** Puzzle reveals, highlight moments, "Can you find the move?"
- **Hook window:** First 5 seconds
- **Algorithm signal:** Watch time, likes, subscriptions from Short
- **Tips:**
  - End with the answer after building suspense
  - Use closed captions — many watch muted
  - OTB (over-the-board) footage outperforms digital board clips

### Instagram Reels
- **Best content:** Visually striking sets, raw tournament moments, personality clips
- **Hook window:** 3–8 seconds
- **Algorithm signal:** Watch time to completion + saves
- **Tips:**
  - Aesthetic board setups perform better than screen recordings
  - Raw/authentic moments outperform polished production
  - Strong first frame — bold text or unexpected position

## Content Calendar Template

### Weekly Output Target
- **3x Puzzle shorts** — "Can you find the mate in 2?" format
- **2x Blunder/highlight clips** — OTB or top-level games
- **1x Trending moment** — react to viral chess news or streamer clip
- **1x Educational hook** — trick opening or opening trap (keeps beginners)

### Daily Posting Times (general best practice)
- TikTok: 7–9am or 7–9pm local audience time
- YouTube Shorts: 12pm–3pm
- Instagram Reels: 6am–9am or 12pm–2pm

---

## Hooks That Work

Use one of these in the first 3 seconds:

- `"99% of players miss this..."`
- `"White to move. Can you find it?"`
- `"This move ended his career."`
- `"The most embarrassing blunder in chess history."`
- `"Magnus did NOT see this coming."`
- `"This trick wins in 4 moves every time."`

## What to Avoid

- Long opening theory explanations (low retention, low shares)
- Overly polished production — raw beats refined on short-form
- No hook in the first 3 seconds
- Videos over 60 seconds unless story is exceptional
- Digital board only — mix in real board footage
